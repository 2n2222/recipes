Ingredients

8 green bell peppers, tops cut away and seeds removed

2 tablespoons vegetable oil

1  finely chopped  red onion

1 8oz container of baby bellas sliced

2 lbs of  93 %  ground beef

4 cloves minced garlic

1/2 tsp of oregano

3/4 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

1/2 tsp red pepper flakes

1/2 tsp thyme

1/2 cup grated romano cheese

2 cups cooked long or medium-grain white rice

8 ounces tomato sauce

2 jars of tomato/pasta sauce

Directions

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.

In a large pot of boiling water, parboil the peppers until just tender, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and dry on paper towels.

In a large saute pan or skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the onions and mushrooms and cook, stirring, until soft, about 3 minutes. Add the beef, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, black pepper.

Cook until the meat is browned, stirring with a heavy wooden spoon to break up the lumps, about 6 minutes.

Add the rice, dried pepper flakes, romano cheese and tomato sauce and stir well. Remove from the heat and adjust the seasoning, to taste. Pour enough tomato/pasta sauce into a baking dish to just cover the bottom, about 1/8-inch deep. Stuff the bell peppers with the rice mixture and place in the baking dish. Pour over more tomato sauce and  Bake until the peppers are very tender and the filling is heated through, 25 to 30 minutes.

Remove from the oven and let rest for 10 minutes before serving.
